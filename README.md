# Orkaanyc
**A constructed language focused on precision over amiguity.**

## Introduction
Orkaanyc is definetly a work-in-progress. I started this project in Fall of 2025, originally as a language to write my diary in. The language prioritises precision over ambiguity; I'd often take a look at things I don't like in English and change them here. The language is meant to have a sort of Nothern European sound to it, and it was partially inspired by videos of people speaking Old English. I also eventually decided to write lore around the fictional speakers of the language, which I will include in this repository. Also, for ease of writing with US keyboards and old technology, Orkaanyc only uses plain Latin characters, and other than the IPA, should be completely writable in ASCII.

## Fileguide
### README.md
The file you are reading right now. Will get updated with every major update of Orkaanyc or so.
### PHONOLOGY.txt
The Phonology of Orkaanyc, with both IPA and examples of sounds in English. I imagine it won't change much other than updates to pronounciation based on accent and fixing typos.
### PARTICLES.txt
A list of Particles in Orkaanyc. Will get updated as new particles are added. Also needs more consistant formatting
### ESSENTIAL-WORDS.txt
A list of basic words with specific grammatical functions such as the corresponding words for "what", "if", "because", "or", "to", "some", etc. This file will likely later get divided into several smaller files based on grammatical function, but for now it's just a way to keep track of important words. It currently is still pretty messy, at least in terms of formatting.
