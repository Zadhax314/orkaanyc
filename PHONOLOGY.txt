VOWELS
RED VOWELS

These are mostly A-related or “open” vowels.

aa

"ay" sound, as in "day" /eɪ/

a

Default: "ah" sound, as in "aqua" /a/

Between two consonants: "a" sound, as in "apple"

ea

Always "ah" sound, as in "aqua" /æ/

au

Always "a" sound as in "apple" /a/

ie

"ai" sound, as in "Kai" /aɪ/ (NOT like English "pike")

ol

"o" sound as in "lot" /ɑ/

The "l" is pronounced unless followed by a consonant

BLUE VOWELS

These are mostly "round" or "back" vowels.

oo

"oo" as in "good" /ʊ/

o

"oh" as in "go" /o/

ou

"uh" sound as in "up" /ʌ/

u

"oo" as in "goo" /u/

y

"ih" as in the Y in "Sydney" /ɪ/

At word-end: closer to a Russian-style hard "y"

w 

"wu", with the "u" barely audible /u̯/

YELLOW VOWELS

These are mostly "light" or neutral vowels.

e

"e" as in "egg" /ɛ/

ey

unstressed vowel like the a in "about" or "sofa" /ə/

i

"ee" as in "Wii" /i/

CONSONANTS
SIMPLE CONSONANTS (single letters)

b – same as English /b/
d - same as English /d/
f – same as English /f/ 
g – soft "j" sound in the middle /dʒ/; "ng" at word-end /ŋ/. Hard at the beginning always /g/
h – mostly used in digraphs, but can make the same sound as in English /h/
j – "y" as in "yellow" /j/
k – same as English /k/
l – same as English /l/
m – same as English /m/
n – same as English /n/
p – same as English /p/ 
r – same as English /ɹ/
s – "z" only at word-end /z/ ; "s" elsewhere /s/
t – same as English /t/ 
x – "ks" /ks/
z – same as English /z/

DOUBLE / COMPLEX CONSONANTS
cc

Always "ch" /tʃ/

Only mid or end of word

(Single "c" is also "ch", except at word-end)

c (end of word only)

"k" /k/ 

dd

"nd" sound at end of word /nd/

dj

"j" sound as in "judge" /dʒ/

fh

"th" as in "thief" /θ/

gg

Hard "g" as in "ghost", mid or end /ɡ/ 

hj

Always normal "h" sound, but uncommon. /h/ 

sc

"sh" sound /ʃ/

sj

"zh" sound, as in French "Jacques" /ʒ/

th

"th" as in "the" /ð/ 

xx

"sk" /sk/

SPECIAL RHOTIC COMBINATIONS


or – "or" /oɹ/
ar – "ar" /aɹ/
er – "air" /ɛɹ/
yr – "er" /ɝ/
ir – "ear"/iɹ/
